CELPIP Speaking Task 1 is your first impression — and it's one of the easiest tasks to master with the right template. You have **90 seconds** to give advice to a friend about a specific situation. Here's exactly how to nail it.

## What to Expect

You'll see a prompt like:

> *"A friend is considering quitting their full-time job to start a small business. Give them advice about what they should consider."*

You get **30 seconds** to prepare, then **90 seconds** to speak. The screen shows the prompt the entire time.

## The Perfect Template for Task 1

Use this 3-part structure to fill 90 seconds every time:

### Opening (10-15 seconds)
> "Hey [name], that's a really interesting situation, and I completely understand why you're thinking about this. I have a few suggestions that I think could really help you out."

### Body — 3 Pieces of Advice (50-60 seconds)

**Advice 1 (15-20 sec):**
> "First of all, I would strongly recommend that you [specific advice]. The reason I say this is because [explanation]. For example, [brief example or detail]."

**Advice 2 (15-20 sec):**
> "Secondly, another important thing to consider is [advice]. This is crucial because [reason]. I actually know someone who [brief anecdote], and it really made a difference."

**Advice 3 (15-20 sec):**
> "Finally, I'd also suggest that you [advice]. This might seem minor, but [why it matters]. Trust me, you'll be glad you did."

### Closing (10-15 seconds)
> "Overall, I really believe that if you follow these steps, things will work out well for you. I'm always here if you need to talk more about it. Good luck!"

## Sample High-Scoring Response

**Prompt:** *A friend just moved to a new city and doesn't know anyone. Give them advice on how to make new friends.*

> "Hey Sarah, first of all, congratulations on the move! I know it can feel overwhelming to be in a new city where you don't know anyone, but I have some great suggestions for you.

> First, I would strongly recommend joining some local clubs or groups that match your interests. Whether it's a hiking group, a book club, or even a cooking class — these are fantastic ways to meet like-minded people. You'll have something in common right from the start, which makes conversation so much easier.

> Secondly, I'd suggest checking out community events in your neighbourhood. Most cities have weekend markets, festivals, or volunteer opportunities. Volunteering is especially wonderful because the people who volunteer tend to be friendly and open to meeting new people.

> Finally, don't underestimate the power of your workplace. Try inviting a colleague for coffee or lunch. Even a simple 'Hey, do you know any good restaurants around here?' can spark a genuine friendship.

> Overall, the key is putting yourself out there consistently. It takes time, but I promise it gets easier. You're going to love your new city!"

**Why this scores CLB 9+:**
- ✅ Uses the friend's name (engagement)
- ✅ 3 clear, distinct pieces of advice
- ✅ Specific examples for each
- ✅ Natural transitions ("First," "Secondly," "Finally")
- ✅ Fills the full 90 seconds
- ✅ Warm, encouraging closing
- ✅ Advanced vocabulary: "overwhelming," "like-minded," "genuine," "consistently"

## Common Task 1 Mistakes

### ❌ Giving Only One Piece of Advice
One point = ~30 seconds. You have 90 seconds. That's 60 seconds of silence or painful repetition. **Always prepare 3 pieces of advice.**

### ❌ Being Too Vague
"I think you should be careful" → CLB 6
"I'd recommend researching at least three different options online and comparing their reviews" → CLB 9

**Specific details = higher scores.**

### ❌ Forgetting the Friend Element
This is advice to a **friend**, not a formal presentation. Use their name, show empathy, be encouraging. "I totally get how you feel" sounds natural. "One must consider the implications" sounds robotic.

### ❌ Not Using Preparation Time
You get 30 seconds to prepare. Use ALL of it:
- Seconds 1-10: Read the prompt carefully
- Seconds 11-25: Plan your 3 advice points (jot mental notes)
- Seconds 26-30: Think of your opening line

## Power Phrases for Task 1

Keep these in your back pocket:

**Opening empathy:**
- "I completely understand what you're going through..."
- "That's a tough situation, but I have some ideas..."
- "I've actually been in a similar situation, so let me share..."

**Giving advice:**
- "I would strongly recommend..."
- "One thing that I think would really help is..."
- "Have you considered...? I think it could make a huge difference."

**Adding examples:**
- "For instance, a friend of mine..."
- "I remember when I..."
- "Studies have shown that..."

**Closing:**
- "I'm confident things will work out if you..."
- "Don't hesitate to reach out if you need more help."
- "You've got this — I believe in you!"
