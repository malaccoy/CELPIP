Your CELPIP test is in one week. Don't panic — you can still make significant improvements with a focused, strategic study plan. This crash course targets the **highest-impact areas** for rapid score improvement.

## Before You Start: Quick Assessment

Before diving in, take 15 minutes to identify your weak spots:

1. **[Take our free CLB assessment →](/dashboard)** to get your baseline level
2. Note which sections score lowest — that's where you focus first
3. Be honest about your weaknesses (most students overestimate their Speaking and Writing)

**The 80/20 rule:** Spend 80% of your time on your **two weakest sections** and 20% maintaining your strong ones.

## Day 1 (Monday): Writing Mastery

**Time: 2-3 hours**

### Morning: Task 1 — Email Writing (90 min)
1. **Learn the template** (15 min): Read our [Writing Score 9+ guide](/blog/celpip-writing-score-9) and memorize the email template
2. **Practice 2 timed emails** (54 min): Write each in 27 minutes — strict timer!
3. **Review with AI feedback** (20 min): Submit to our [AI Writing Tutor →](/writing/task-1) and study the feedback

### Afternoon: Task 2 — Survey Response (90 min)
1. **Learn the template** (15 min): Memorize the survey response structure
2. **Practice 2 timed responses** (52 min): 26 minutes each
3. **Build your vocabulary bank** (20 min): Note 10 advanced words from the AI feedback and practice using them

**Day 1 Vocabulary Goal:** Learn these transitions — *furthermore, consequently, nevertheless, moreover, in addition, specifically, particularly*

> **[Practice Writing Task 1 →](/writing/task-1)** | **[Practice Writing Task 2 →](/writing/task-2)**

## Day 2 (Tuesday): Speaking Foundation

**Time: 2-3 hours**

### Morning: Templates & Practice (90 min)
1. **Memorize universal template** (20 min): Opening → Point 1 + Detail → Point 2 + Detail → Closing
2. **Practice Tasks 1, 2, 3** (45 min): Record yourself, review, repeat
3. **Pronunciation drill** (25 min): Practice word stress patterns and th/r/l sounds

### Afternoon: Advanced Tasks (90 min)
1. **Practice Tasks 5, 6, 7** (45 min): These are the hardest — persuading, complaining, opinions
2. **Vocabulary expansion** (20 min): Replace "good/bad/important" with advanced synonyms
3. **Record and self-evaluate** (25 min): Listen back — are you filling the full time? Any long pauses?

**Day 2 Goal:** Be able to speak for the full duration of every task without going silent.

> **[Practice with AI Speaking Coach →](/speaking/practice)**

## Day 3 (Wednesday): Listening Strategy

**Time: 2 hours**

### Full Practice Session (120 min)
1. **Read-before-listen drill** (30 min): Practice reading questions quickly and identifying keywords BEFORE playing audio
2. **Practice all 6 part types** (60 min): Use our [Listening practice →](/listening/practice) with one-play audio
3. **Review mistakes** (30 min): For every wrong answer, go back and identify WHY you missed it:
   - Didn't hear a key word? → Vocabulary issue
   - Heard it but chose wrong? → Comprehension issue
   - Ran out of time? → Speed issue

**Day 3 Listening Hacks:**
- Keywords in questions are your GPS — listen specifically for them
- If two answers seem correct, the more specific one is usually right
- "All of the above" is rarely the answer on CELPIP
- Pay attention to tone — sarcasm and emphasis change meaning

## Day 4 (Thursday): Reading Speed

**Time: 2 hours**

### Speed Reading Practice (120 min)
1. **Skim-and-scan drill** (40 min): Practice reading questions first, then scanning the passage for answers
2. **Time yourself on all 4 parts** (60 min): Aim for 13-15 minutes per part — use our [Reading practice →](/reading/practice)
3. **"Almost right" elimination** (20 min): For questions you got wrong, study the distractors — learn to spot "almost right" answers that are actually wrong

**Day 4 Reading Strategy:**
- Part 1 (Correspondence): Focus on the PURPOSE of each email
- Part 2 (Diagram): Match text details to visual elements precisely
- Part 3 (Information): Skim for structure first (headings, first sentences of paragraphs)
- Part 4 (Viewpoints): Track which author says what — they disagree!

## Day 5 (Friday): Weak Section Deep Dive

**Time: 3 hours**

Today is entirely dedicated to your **weakest section**. Whatever scored lowest on Day 1's assessment:

### If Writing is weakest:
- Write 4 more timed responses (2 emails + 2 surveys)
- Focus on the AI feedback: what errors keep repeating?
- Memorize 5 new advanced collocations

### If Speaking is weakest:
- Record 8 practice responses (one per task type)
- Listen back to every recording — identify patterns
- Practice filler phrases: "What I mean by that is..." "Let me elaborate..."

### If Listening is weakest:
- Do 3 full Listening practice sets
- Practice note-taking on the erasable notepad
- Watch Canadian news (CBC) for 30 minutes — train your ear

### If Reading is weakest:
- Do 3 full Reading practice sets with strict time limits
- Practice paragraph-first reading (read first sentence of each paragraph to get structure)
- Work on vocabulary — unknown words slow you down

## Day 6 (Saturday): Full Simulation

**Time: 3 hours**

### Mock Test (180 min)
1. Simulate the real test environment:
   - Find a quiet room
   - No phone, no interruptions
   - Use a timer for each section
   - No breaks between sections (or only the breaks CELPIP allows)

2. Take the full mock exam:
   - Listening: ~50 min
   - Reading: ~55 min
   - Writing: ~53 min
   - Speaking: ~20 min

3. **Score yourself honestly.** Use the AI evaluations for Writing and Speaking.

4. **Identify final weak spots** — these are your targets for tomorrow.

> **[Take a Mock Exam →](/dashboard)** with AI scoring and CLB estimate.

## Day 7 (Sunday — Test Eve): Light Review

**Time: 1-2 hours only**

### DO:
- Review your templates one last time (don't try to learn new ones)
- Review your vocabulary bank — read through your advanced words list
- Do 1-2 light practice exercises just to stay sharp
- Prepare your test-day kit: valid ID, test centre address, comfortable clothes
- Go to bed early — sleep is more valuable than extra study

### DON'T:
- Cram new material (your brain needs time to consolidate)
- Practice for more than 2 hours (diminishing returns + fatigue)
- Stay up late studying (lack of sleep hurts performance more than any amount of studying helps)
- Panic (you've put in the work — trust the preparation)

## Test Day Checklist

- ☐ Valid photo ID (passport preferred)
- ☐ Test centre address (arrive 30 minutes early)
- ☐ Comfortable clothing (you'll sit for 3 hours)
- ☐ Water bottle (if allowed at your centre)
- ☐ Confidence (you've prepared — now execute)

**During the test:**
- Use ALL available time for each section
- Don't rush — accuracy beats speed
- In Speaking, NEVER go silent — say anything
- In Writing, leave 2-3 minutes to review for typos
- In Listening, read questions BEFORE the audio starts
- In Reading, answer easy questions first, skip and return to hard ones

## One Week From Now

If you follow this plan consistently, you can realistically improve **1-2 CLB levels** in a week. The students who improve most are the ones who:

1. Identified their specific weak spots
2. Practiced with timed conditions
3. Got feedback on every practice attempt
4. Fixed specific errors instead of "studying generally"
