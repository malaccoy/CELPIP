Vocabulary is worth **25% of your Writing score** and heavily impacts Speaking. The difference between CLB 7 and CLB 9 is often just 30-40 upgraded words. This curated list gives you the highest-impact vocabulary for each CELPIP section.

## How to Use This List

Don't just read — **practice actively**:
1. Learn 10 words per day (not 50 at once)
2. Write a sentence using each word
3. Say each sentence out loud (trains Speaking too)
4. Review yesterday's words before learning new ones
5. Use them in your CELPIP practice responses

## Part 1: Replace Basic Words

The fastest vocabulary upgrade — stop using these basic words:

| ❌ Basic (CLB 6-7) | ✅ Advanced (CLB 9+) | Example |
|---|---|---|
| good | exceptional, remarkable, outstanding | "An exceptional opportunity" |
| bad | detrimental, concerning, problematic | "A detrimental impact" |
| big | substantial, significant, considerable | "A substantial improvement" |
| small | minimal, negligible, marginal | "A negligible difference" |
| important | crucial, essential, paramount | "A crucial factor" |
| a lot | considerably, substantially, extensively | "Improved considerably" |
| very | remarkably, exceedingly, profoundly | "Remarkably effective" |
| get | obtain, acquire, attain | "Obtain permission" |
| make | construct, generate, establish | "Establish a routine" |
| give | provide, contribute, allocate | "Allocate resources" |
| show | demonstrate, illustrate, indicate | "Demonstrate competence" |
| help | facilitate, assist, contribute to | "Facilitate learning" |
| think | consider, maintain, contend | "I firmly maintain" |
| use | utilize, employ, leverage | "Leverage technology" |
| like | appreciate, value, favour | "I greatly appreciate" |
| need | require, necessitate, demand | "This necessitates" |
| try | attempt, endeavour, strive | "Strive for excellence" |

| change | transform, modify, revolutionize | "Transform the approach" |

## Part 2: Transition & Linking Words

These connect your ideas smoothly — essential for both Writing and Speaking coherence scores.

### Adding Information
- Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally, In addition to this
- What's more, On top of that, Not only... but also
- "Furthermore, the evidence suggests..."

### Contrasting
- However, Nevertheless, Nonetheless, On the other hand
- Despite this, In contrast, Conversely, Although
- "Nevertheless, the benefits outweigh the costs."

### Cause & Effect
- Consequently, Therefore, As a result, Hence
- This leads to, This results in, Due to, Owing to
- "Consequently, productivity has increased significantly."

### Giving Examples
- For instance, For example, To illustrate, Such as
- A case in point is, This is exemplified by
- "For instance, studies conducted in 2025 revealed..."

### Concluding
- In conclusion, To summarize, All things considered
- Weighing all factors, Ultimately, On the whole
- "All things considered, the advantages clearly outweigh the disadvantages."

### Emphasizing
- Particularly, Especially, Notably, Significantly
- It's worth noting that, What's crucial is
- "This is particularly relevant for..."

## Part 3: Topic-Specific Vocabulary

### Workplace & Career
- **Nouns:** productivity, efficiency, collaboration, initiative, deadline, delegation, innovation, accountability
- **Verbs:** implement, streamline, prioritize, coordinate, delegate, supervise, optimize
- **Collocations:** "meet a deadline," "take initiative," "boost productivity," "foster collaboration"

### Community & Society
- **Nouns:** infrastructure, demographic, initiative, diversity, well-being, sustainability, engagement
- **Verbs:** revitalize, advocate, implement, allocate, address, promote
- **Collocations:** "community engagement," "address the needs of," "promote well-being," "allocate funding"

### Education & Learning
- **Nouns:** curriculum, literacy, accessibility, pedagogy, scholarship, proficiency
- **Verbs:** cultivate, acquire, enhance, evaluate, facilitate, mentor
- **Collocations:** "cultivate critical thinking," "enhance proficiency," "bridge the gap"

### Health & Lifestyle
- **Nouns:** well-being, nutrition, fitness, mindfulness, prevention, resilience
- **Verbs:** maintain, alleviate, prevent, promote, prioritize
- **Collocations:** "maintain a healthy lifestyle," "alleviate stress," "promote mental health"

### Technology
- **Nouns:** innovation, automation, connectivity, cybersecurity, sustainability, efficiency
- **Verbs:** revolutionize, integrate, automate, optimize, streamline
- **Collocations:** "leverage technology," "digital transformation," "technological advancement"

### Environment
- **Nouns:** sustainability, conservation, biodiversity, ecosystem, carbon footprint, emissions
- **Verbs:** mitigate, conserve, reduce, implement, advocate
- **Collocations:** "mitigate climate change," "reduce carbon footprint," "promote sustainability"

## Part 4: Power Phrases for Speaking

Memorize these and plug them into any Speaking task:

### Expressing Strong Opinions
- "I'm thoroughly convinced that..."
- "I wholeheartedly support..."
- "There's compelling evidence to suggest..."
- "I firmly maintain that..."

### Hedging (Softening)
- "It seems reasonable to conclude..."
- "There's a strong possibility that..."
- "In most cases, this tends to..."
- "While I can't be entirely certain..."

### Comparing
- "This far surpasses..."
- "In comparison with..."
- "The advantages significantly outweigh..."
- "This pales in comparison to..."

### Advising (Task 1)
- "I would strongly recommend..."
- "It might be worth considering..."
- "One approach that could prove beneficial is..."
- "Have you given any thought to...?"

## Part 5: Collocations (Word Pairs)

Collocations sound natural; wrong combinations sound awkward. Learn these pairs:

| ✅ Natural | ❌ Unnatural |
|---|---|
| make a decision | do a decision |
| do research | make research |
| take responsibility | get responsibility |
| pay attention | give attention |
| reach a conclusion | arrive to a conclusion |
| raise awareness | lift awareness |
| meet requirements | fill requirements |
| gain experience | win experience |
| pose a threat | make a threat |
| draw a conclusion | pull a conclusion |
| break a habit | destroy a habit |
| face challenges | meet challenges |
| set a goal | put a goal |
| catch someone's attention | grab someone's eye |
| make progress | do progress |

## Daily Vocabulary Routine (15 minutes)

1. **Morning (5 min):** Review 5 words from yesterday + learn 5 new ones
2. **Afternoon (5 min):** Write 5 sentences using today's words
3. **Evening (5 min):** Say each sentence aloud (Speaking practice)

In 30 days, you'll have 150 new active vocabulary words — more than enough for CLB 9+.
