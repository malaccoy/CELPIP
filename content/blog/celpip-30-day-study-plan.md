Thirty days is the sweet spot for CELPIP preparation — enough time to make real improvements without burning out. This plan is designed for 2-3 hours of daily practice and targets CLB 9+ across all sections.

## Before You Start: Baseline Assessment

**Day 0 (Today):** Take a [free CLB assessment →](/dashboard) to identify your starting level. Record your scores:

| Section | My Starting CLB | Target CLB | Gap |
|---------|----------------|-----------|-----|
| Listening | ___ | 9+ | ___ |
| Reading | ___ | 9+ | ___ |
| Writing | ___ | 9+ | ___ |
| Speaking | ___ | 9+ | ___ |

**Allocate your time** based on gaps:
- 3+ level gap → This section gets 40% of your daily time
- 2 level gap → 30%
- 1 level gap → 20%
- At target → 10% (maintenance only)

## Week 1: Foundation (Days 1-7)

**Goal:** Learn the test format, build templates, establish habits.

### Day 1: Test Format Deep Dive
- Read the [Complete CELPIP Format Guide →](/blog/celpip-test-format-2026) (30 min)
- Watch official CELPIP sample test videos on YouTube (30 min)
- Take notes on timing, question types, and interface (15 min)
- Complete 1 practice exercise in each section (60 min)

### Day 2: Writing Templates
- Read the [Writing Score 9+ Guide →](/blog/celpip-writing-score-9) (20 min)
- Memorize the formal email template (20 min)
- Write 1 timed Task 1 email (27 min) → [Practice here →](/writing/task-1)
- Review AI feedback and note errors (15 min)
- Memorize the survey response template (20 min)
- Write 1 timed Task 2 response (26 min) → [Practice here →](/writing/task-2)

### Day 3: Speaking Templates
- Read the [Speaking Score 9+ Guide →](/blog/celpip-speaking-score-9) (20 min)
- Memorize the universal speaking template (20 min)
- Practice Tasks 1, 3, 5, 7 with recording (60 min)
- Listen back and identify weaknesses (20 min)

### Day 4: Listening Strategies
- Read the [Listening Tips Guide →](/blog/celpip-listening-tips-score-9) (15 min)
- Practice the read-first technique with 3 audio clips (45 min)
- Complete one full Listening practice set (50 min) → [Practice here →](/listening/practice)
- Review wrong answers — identify WHY you missed each one (20 min)

### Day 5: Reading Strategies
- Read the [Reading Tips Guide →](/blog/celpip-reading-tips-score-9) (15 min)
- Practice questions-first approach with 2 passages (30 min)
- Complete one full Reading practice set (55 min) → [Practice here →](/reading/practice)
- Review: practice spotting "almost right" answer traps (20 min)

### Day 6: Vocabulary Day
- Study the [200 Essential Words list →](/blog/celpip-vocabulary-list-200-words) (30 min)
- Learn 20 word replacements (basic → advanced) (30 min)
- Write 10 sentences using new words (20 min)
- Say each sentence aloud (10 min)
- Practice a Writing Task 1 using at least 5 new words (30 min)

### Day 7: Week 1 Review
- Retake the free assessment — compare to Day 0 scores
- Identify which section improved most and which needs more work
- Adjust Week 2 time allocation based on results
- Rest in the evening — your brain needs consolidation time

## Week 2: Skill Building (Days 8-14)

**Goal:** Deepen strategies, increase practice volume, build stamina.

### Daily Routine (2.5 hours)
| Block | Time | Activity |
|-------|------|----------|
| Warm-up | 15 min | Review 10 vocabulary words + yesterday's errors |
| Weakness Focus | 60 min | Your weakest section — intensive practice |
| Second Section | 45 min | Your second-weakest section |
| Mixed Practice | 30 min | Rotate through other sections |

### Week 2 Goals
- [ ] Write 6 timed responses (3 Task 1 + 3 Task 2) with AI feedback
- [ ] Record 16 Speaking responses (2 per task type) and self-evaluate
- [ ] Complete 3 full Listening practice sets
- [ ] Complete 3 full Reading practice sets
- [ ] Learn 40 new vocabulary words (10/day × 4 days)
- [ ] Memorize all 8 Speaking task templates

### Day 10: Mid-Week Check
Take a practice Listening + Reading set under strict timed conditions. Score yourself. Are you improving? Adjust focus if needed.

## Week 3: Refinement (Days 15-21)

**Goal:** Fine-tune weak areas, reduce errors, polish templates.

### Daily Routine (2.5 hours)
| Block | Time | Activity |
|-------|------|----------|
| Error Review | 15 min | Review yesterday's mistakes — never repeat them |
| Targeted Practice | 75 min | Focus on specific sub-skills (see below) |
| Full Section Practice | 45 min | Complete timed section practice |
| Self-Assessment | 15 min | Record what's improving and what's stuck |

### Sub-Skill Focus Areas

**If Writing is weak:**
- Day 15: Practice tone switching (formal → informal → semi-formal)
- Day 16: Focus on paragraph transitions and coherence
- Day 17: Grammar intensive — complex sentences, relative clauses
- Day 18: Vocabulary precision — use collocations from your word bank

**If Speaking is weak:**
- Day 15: Pronunciation drills — word stress, th/r/l sounds
- Day 16: Fluency practice — eliminate filler words and long pauses
- Day 17: Content development — practice elaborating on points
- Day 18: Timed runs — fill every second without rushing

**If Listening is weak:**
- Day 15: Speed listening — practice at 1.25x, then normal feels easy
- Day 16: Detail catching — focus on numbers, names, dates
- Day 17: Inference practice — what's implied vs stated?
- Day 18: Note-taking efficiency — develop shorthand system

**If Reading is weak:**
- Day 15: Speed reading drills — timed paragraph summaries
- Day 16: Vocabulary expansion — learn academic word list
- Day 17: Answer elimination — practice crossing out wrong answers
- Day 18: Part 4 focus — viewpoint comparison (hardest part)

### Day 21: Week 3 Assessment
Full-length mock test simulation. All 4 sections, real timing, no breaks. Score everything. Compare to Day 0 and Day 7.

## Week 4: Peak Performance (Days 22-28)

**Goal:** Simulate test conditions, build confidence, peak on test day.

### Days 22-24: Intensive Practice
- 3 hours daily on your two weakest sections
- Every practice is timed and scored
- Keep an error journal — write down every mistake and the correction
- Review the error journal before each practice session

### Day 25: Full Mock Test #2
- Simulate exact test conditions (3 hours, no phone, quiet room)
- Score yourself honestly
- This is your "dress rehearsal"

### Day 26: Targeted Fixes
- Only work on mistakes from yesterday's mock test
- Don't learn anything new — refine what you have
- Practice your Speaking templates 3 more times each

### Day 27: Light Review
- 1 hour maximum
- Review vocabulary bank, templates, and strategies
- Do 2-3 easy practice exercises to stay sharp
- Prepare test-day logistics (ID, route to test centre, comfortable clothes)

### Day 28: Rest Day
- **No studying.** Seriously.
- Your brain needs 24 hours to consolidate everything
- Do something relaxing — walk, movie, cook, whatever calms you
- Go to bed early — sleep is your final performance booster

## Test Day (Day 29-30)

- Arrive 30 minutes early
- Do a quick mental review of templates while waiting
- Take deep breaths before each section
- Trust your preparation — you've put in 70+ hours
