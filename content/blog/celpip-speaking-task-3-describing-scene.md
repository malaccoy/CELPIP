CELPIP Speaking Task 3 shows you an image and gives you **60 seconds** to describe it. It's one of the easiest tasks to score well on — if you have a systematic approach. Here's the template that guarantees you fill the time with high-quality content.

## What to Expect

You'll see a detailed image on screen — it could be:
- A park scene with people doing activities
- A busy restaurant or café
- An office or workplace
- A street scene or market
- A family gathering or event

You get **30 seconds** to study the image, then **60 seconds** to describe it.

## The 4-Layer Description Template

Describe the image in layers — from big picture to small details:

### Layer 1: Overview (10 seconds)
> "In this image, I can see [overall scene — what, where]. It appears to be [time/setting/atmosphere]."

### Layer 2: Main Subjects (15-20 seconds)
> "In the center / foreground, there is [main person/activity]. They appear to be [action/emotion]. Next to them, I can see [another person/element]."

### Layer 3: Supporting Details (15-20 seconds)
> "On the left/right side, I notice [detail]. In the background, there's [detail]. I can also see [additional element], which suggests [interpretation]."

### Layer 4: Atmosphere & Impression (10 seconds)
> "Overall, the scene gives a [adjective] impression. It seems like [interpretation — people are enjoying themselves / it's a busy day / etc.]."

## Sample Response

**Image:** A busy farmers' market with vendors, shoppers, and various stalls.

> "In this image, I can see a vibrant outdoor farmers' market that appears to be taking place on a beautiful sunny morning.

> In the foreground, there's a young woman carefully selecting fresh tomatoes from a colourful vegetable stall. She's holding a reusable cloth bag and seems to be examining each tomato quite thoughtfully. Right beside her, an elderly gentleman is chatting enthusiastically with the vendor, who's wearing a bright green apron.

> On the left side of the image, I notice a flower stall with stunning bouquets of sunflowers and roses, attracting several shoppers who are admiring the arrangements. In the background, there are additional stalls selling what appears to be artisan bread and homemade jams. There are also families with children walking between the stalls, and I can spot a small dog on a leash near one of the tables.

> Overall, the scene conveys a wonderful sense of community and warmth. Everyone appears to be thoroughly enjoying their Saturday morning at the market, and the atmosphere feels incredibly welcoming and lively."

**Why this scores CLB 9+:**
- ✅ Organized from overview → foreground → sides → background
- ✅ Rich spatial vocabulary (foreground, beside, left side, background)
- ✅ Descriptive adjectives (vibrant, colourful, stunning, enthusiastically)
- ✅ Inferences about people's emotions and the atmosphere
- ✅ Fills the full 60 seconds naturally
- ✅ Strong closing that summarizes the mood

## Essential Spatial Vocabulary

### Location Words
| Position | Phrases |
|----------|---------|
| Center | "In the center of the image," "The focal point is" |
| Foreground | "In the foreground," "Up close, I can see" |
| Background | "In the background," "Further away" |
| Left/Right | "On the left-hand side," "To the right" |
| Between | "Between the two [items]," "Sandwiched between" |
| Above/Below | "Above them," "At the bottom of the image" |

### Action Words
- Walking, running, sitting, standing, leaning
- Chatting, laughing, pointing, examining, browsing
- Carrying, holding, reaching for, pushing, pulling
- Preparing, cooking, serving, cleaning, arranging

### Atmosphere Words
- Lively, bustling, peaceful, serene, chaotic
- Welcoming, cozy, professional, festive, relaxed
- Crowded, spacious, colourful, bright, dimly lit

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Just listing items
"I see a man. I see a woman. I see a dog. I see a tree." → CLB 5

### ✅ Connecting and describing
"A middle-aged man in a blue jacket is walking his golden retriever near a large oak tree, and they both seem to be enjoying the crisp autumn weather." → CLB 9

### ❌ Stopping at 30 seconds
You have 60 seconds. Use ALL of them. Add interpretations, emotions, atmosphere, and details.

### ❌ Ignoring the background
The background often contains important details. Describing it shows thorough observation.

## 30-Second Preparation Strategy

1. **Seconds 1-10:** Scan the ENTIRE image — what's the overall scene?
2. **Seconds 11-20:** Identify 3-4 main elements to describe
3. **Seconds 21-30:** Think of 2-3 adjectives and your closing impression
