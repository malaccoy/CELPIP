## How Much Does the CELPIP Test Cost in 2026?

If you're preparing for Canadian immigration, citizenship, or professional designation, you've probably heard of **CELPIP** (Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program). And one of the first questions everyone asks is: *"How much does it cost?"*

Here's the straightforward answer:

| Test Type | Fee (CAD) |
|-----------|-----------|
| **CELPIP-General** | ~$340 - $390 |
| **CELPIP-General LS** | ~$280 - $340 |

*Note: Prices may vary slightly depending on your location and any applicable taxes. Always check the [official CELPIP website](https://www.celpip.ca) for the most current pricing.*

Let's break down everything you need to know about CELPIP costs, what you're paying for, and how to save money on your preparation.

## CELPIP-General vs. CELPIP-General LS: Which One Do You Need?

Before you register, make sure you're signing up for the **right version** of the test.

### CELPIP-General

This is the **full test** with all four components:

- Listening
- Reading
- Writing
- Speaking

**You need CELPIP-General for:**
- **Express Entry** (Federal Skilled Worker, Canadian Experience Class, Federal Skilled Trades)
- **Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)**
- **Some professional designations**

### CELPIP-General LS (Listening and Speaking)

This is the **shorter version** with only two components:

- Listening
- Speaking

**You need CELPIP-General LS for:**
- **Canadian citizenship applications**

If you're applying for **permanent residency through Express Entry**, you need the full CELPIP-General. Don't make the expensive mistake of booking the wrong test.

### What's Included in Your Registration

When you pay the test fee, you get:

- **One test sitting** at your selected test centre and date
- **Online access** to your scores (typically available within 4-5 business days)
- **One score report** sent to a designated institution
- **Score validity** for 2 years from the test date

## How to Register for CELPIP

The registration process is entirely online. Here's a step-by-step guide:

### Step 1: Create an Account

Go to **[celpip.ca](https://www.celpip.ca)** and create a free account. You'll need:

- A valid email address
- Your full legal name (must match your ID exactly)
- Your date of birth

### Step 2: Choose Your Test Type

Select either **CELPIP-General** or **CELPIP-General LS** based on your immigration needs.

### Step 3: Select a Test Date and Location

Browse available dates and test centres. **Pro tip:** Book early! Popular test centres in cities like Toronto, Vancouver, and Calgary fill up fast — sometimes weeks in advance.

### Step 4: Pay the Fee

Payment is typically by **credit card or debit card.** Some test centres may offer additional payment options.

### Step 5: Receive Confirmation

You'll get an email confirmation with your test details. **Save this email** — you'll need it on test day.

## Test Centre Locations Across Canada

CELPIP test centres are available in major cities across Canada, including:

- **Ontario:** Toronto, Ottawa, Mississauga, Brampton, Hamilton, London, Waterloo
- **British Columbia:** Vancouver, Surrey, Burnaby, Victoria, Kelowna
- **Alberta:** Calgary, Edmonton
- **Manitoba:** Winnipeg
- **Saskatchewan:** Saskatoon, Regina
- **Atlantic Canada:** Halifax, Moncton, St. John's
- **Quebec:** Montreal (limited availability)

There are also some **international test centres** in countries like India, the UAE, the Philippines, and China. Check the CELPIP website for the most up-to-date list.

### Choosing the Right Test Centre

Some tips for picking your location:

- **Book popular cities early** — Toronto and Vancouver dates go fast
- **Consider smaller cities** — places like Kelowna or Regina often have more availability
- **Check the commute** — you don't want to be stressed about transportation on test day
- **Read reviews** — some centres have better facilities than others

## When Are Test Dates Available?

CELPIP tests are offered **multiple times per week** in most major cities. Unlike some other language tests, you typically don't have to wait months for an available date.

However, availability depends on your location:

- **Major cities (Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary):** Multiple sessions per week
- **Mid-sized cities:** 2-4 sessions per month
- **Smaller cities:** Monthly or bi-monthly

New test dates are released on a **rolling basis.** If you don't see a date that works, check back in a few days.

## Rescheduling and Cancellation Policies

Life happens. Here's what you need to know if your plans change:

### Rescheduling

- You can reschedule your test for a **fee of approximately $50-$75 CAD**
- Rescheduling must be done **at least a certain number of business days before your test date** (check current policy)
- Subject to availability at your preferred test centre

### Cancellation

- Cancellations may receive a **partial refund** depending on how far in advance you cancel
- Last-minute cancellations typically receive **no refund**
- Medical emergencies may be handled on a case-by-case basis with documentation

### No-Shows

If you don't show up on test day, you'll **forfeit your entire fee.** No refund, no reschedule. So if something comes up, always contact CELPIP beforehand.

## Score Reports: What You Need to Know

After you take the test, your scores are typically available online within **4-5 business days.**

### Sending Scores to IRCC

- Your score report can be sent directly to **Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)**
- One report is usually included in your test fee
- Additional reports cost approximately **$30-$40 each**
- Scores are valid for **2 years** from the test date

### Understanding Your Score Report

Your report will show your CLB (Canadian Language Benchmark) level for each section:

- Listening: CLB level
- Reading: CLB level
- Writing: CLB level
- Speaking: CLB level

For Express Entry, you'll input these levels into your profile, and they directly affect your **CRS (Comprehensive Ranking System) score.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I take CELPIP outside Canada?

Yes, but availability is limited. International test centres exist in select countries. Check the CELPIP website for locations.

### How many times can I take the test?

There's **no limit.** You can retake CELPIP as many times as you want, but you'll pay the full fee each time.

### Is CELPIP easier than IELTS?

Many test-takers find CELPIP more comfortable because it's **100% computer-based** (including Speaking, where you talk to a computer rather than a human examiner). However, difficulty is subjective.

### Can I use my CELPIP score for both PR and citizenship?

If you take **CELPIP-General** (the full test), yes — your scores can be used for both permanent residency applications and citizenship. CELPIP-General LS can only be used for citizenship.

### How long should I prepare before taking the test?

Most people benefit from **4-8 weeks** of focused preparation. If your English is already strong, 2-3 weeks might be enough. The key is consistent practice, not cramming.
